## Episode summary

Fr. Luke's ep07 is the public-policy companion to his ep06 doctrinal episode on *Lumen Gentium* §21 and the conflation of order and jurisdiction.
The frame: if Rome had truly retained the perennial doctrine on jurisdiction that ep06 traced, then the 2018 China-Vatican Provisional Agreement, the post-2018 unilateral CPA episcopal appointments that Rome subsequently ratifies, the diplomatic treatment of Sarah Mullally as Archbishop of Canterbury (Leo XIV's March 20, 2026 message), the Bishop of Fresno's active participation in the April 18, 2026 Anglican consecration of Gregory Kimura, the Balamand Declaration's (1993) explicit recognition of Orthodox bishops' jurisdiction over their faithful, and the 2024 Dicastery for Promoting Christian Unity study document *The Bishop of Rome* — would all be either inconceivable or treated as urgent doctrinal emergencies.
They are instead the normal current Roman posture.
The episode's thesis: schism no longer has operative content at Rome, so the charge cannot be wielded coherently against the SSPX consecrations on July 1, 2026.

**6.

Verbatim (Vatican.va Christian Unity archive):

- §14: "the Catholic Churches and the Orthodox Churches recognize each other as Sister Churches"
- §18: quoting Paul VI at the Phanar to Athenagoras (July 25, 1967): heads of the churches should recognize and respect each other "as pastors of that part of the flock of Christ entrusted to them"
- §29: "Bishops and priests have the duty before God to respect the authority which the Holy Spirit has given to the bishops and priests of the other Church and for that reason to avoid interfering in the spiritual life of the faithful of that Church."

Verification status: **fully verified** via the Vatican.va Christian Unity archive direct fetch.

**8.
ARGUMENT / FACT — *The Bishop of Rome: Primacy and Synodality in the Ecumenical Dialogues and in the Responses to the Encyclical 'Ut Unum Sint'***, study document published June 13, 2024 by the Dicastery for Promoting Christian Unity (147 pages, approved by Pope Francis; presented at the Vatican press conference by Cardinal Kurt Koch and Cardinal Mario Grech).
The document explicitly:
- recognizes the *Pastor Aeternus* dogmatic definitions as "a significant obstacle for other Christians with regard to the papacy"
- frames *Lumen Gentium* §21 as having re-established the connection between sacramental and juridical power through ordination, such that "a bishop possesses authority by virtue of his ordination that is not juridically delegated by the bishop of Rome"
- calls for "re-reception" of Vatican I "in light of Vatican II" — the SSPX's structural complaint that the post-conciliar magisterium reads dogmas through the council rather than the other way around
- recommends reframing Roman primacy "not so much as a universal power… as an authority in service to the communion between the Churches"
- concludes with the 2021 plenary assembly proposal "Towards an Exercise of Primacy in the 21st Century"

Verification status: **document existence, date, authorship, length, and substance are fully verified** across NCR, Word on Fire, USCCB, America, Catholic World Report, La Civiltà Cattolica, and the Vatican Christian Unity press release of June 13, 2024.

**9.
ARGUMENT — Pius XI's *Mortalium Animos* (January 6, 1928)** read against the post-conciliar use of the phrase *ut unum sint*.
Pius XI: the only legitimate ecumenism is "ecumenism of return"; the use of the *ut unum sint* phrase by pan-Christian movements is "problematic because it implies that the unity of the Church no longer exists."
John Paul II takes the phrase as the title of his 1995 encyclical — a deliberate inversion.
Substance already in §5 / §6 from the existing *Mortalium Animos* coverage; no new addition needed but the inversion is worth noting in the "Project in the Words of the Popes" sub-section.

### §7 Canon Law — new sub-section after the ep02/ep06 "Historical note on the penalty" and the perennial-order/jurisdiction sub-section

**10.
FACT / ARGUMENT — Lived application of the post-conciliar dispensation: the 2018 China-Vatican Provisional Agreement and its aftermath.**
- **September 22, 2018:** Cardinal Pietro Parolin (Vatican Secretary of State) and the People's Republic of China conclude the *Provisional Agreement Regarding the Appointment of Bishops*.
  The full text has never been published.
  Substance, per Vatican press release: Beijing proposes candidates; the Pope holds a veto.
- **Renewals:** 2020 (two years); 2022 (two years); **October 22, 2024 (four years, extending validity through October 2028)**.
  The agreement is now operative under Pope Leo XIV.
  *(Note: the episode's verbal "into 2029" is a slip; the press release reads "four years from the present date.")*
- **November 24, 2022:** Bishop John Peng Weizhao — originally ordained as bishop of Yujiang on April 10, 2014 with a papal mandate, then arrested by Chinese authorities — is installed in Nanchang as Auxiliary Bishop of Jiangxi, a diocese **not recognized by the Holy See**.
  The Vatican expresses "surprise and regret" and notes the installation did not conform to the 2018 Agreement.
- **April 2023:** Bishop Joseph Shen Bin is transferred unilaterally by the Chinese authorities from the Diocese of Haimen to the Diocese of Shanghai (vacant for ten years) without Vatican involvement; the Holy See learns of the appointment from media accounts.
  **July 15, 2023:** Pope Francis approves the appointment retroactively to "heal the canonical irregularity created by Beijing's unilateral transfer."
  Cardinal Parolin acknowledges the unilateral act "violated the spirit of collaboration."
- **April 2026:** Human Rights Watch publishes "China: Pressure on Catholics Escalates" documenting continued repression of underground Catholic communities under Xi Jinping (in power since November 2012), forced political indoctrination of CPA clergy, and travel restrictions.

The canonical point: Rome retains the 1951 Holy Office decree / 1958 *Ad Apostolorum Principis* / 1983 CIC Canon 1382 (renumbered 1387 in 2021) machinery — but does not apply its discipline to the lived practice of unilateral CPA appointments.
It ratifies them.
The 1951 decree was promulgated *specifically* against the CPA situation.
The episode's structural conclusion is that the discipline is now selectively wielded.

Verification status: **all dates, names, and substance fully verified** via Vatican News, Vatican press releases, AP, NCR, Catholic World Report, Reuters, and Human Rights Watch.

### §10 Case For — Part VIII (Objections) — strengthening the "SSPX is in schism" sub-section with the hypocrisy / structural-inconsistency argument

**11.
ARGUMENT — Three groups Rome currently treats as ecumenically partnered rather than schismatic, despite all three meeting pre-1988 schismatic criteria:**

(a) **Chinese Patriotic Catholic Association** — see §7 above.
Rome ratifies unilateral CPA appointments without papal mandate; the 1951 decree intended to deter exactly this is not enforced against them.

(b) **The Anglican Communion** — Pope Leo XIV's March 20, 2026 message to Sarah Mullally addresses her as "Dear sister" and attributes "responsibilities… throughout the Church of England as well as the Anglican Communion as a whole."
On April 18, 2026, Bishop Joseph Vincent Brennan, Catholic Bishop of the Diocese of Fresno, was present at and participated in the laying-on-of-hands portion of the consecration of Gregory Kimura as the sixth bishop of the Episcopal Diocese of San Joaquin, held at St. James Episcopal Cathedral in Fresno.
*(The Catholic Herald, NCR/Catholic Culture, and Novus Ordo Watch all confirmed the participation from publicly circulated footage; as of late April 2026, neither the Diocese of Fresno nor the Holy See had announced disciplinary review.)* All of this works against *Apostolicae Curae* (Leo XIII, September 15, 1896), which declared Anglican orders "absolutely null and utterly void."
The ARCIC 2006 document explicitly recommends mutual attendance at each other's ordinations.

(c) **The Orthodox churches** — Balamand 1993 §29 verbatim acknowledges Orthodox bishops' jurisdiction over their faithful as given by the Holy Spirit; JPII Yerevan 2001 acknowledges "real and intimate unity" with the Armenian Apostolic Church on the basis of apostolic succession and valid sacraments alone; JPII-Demetrius I 1979 rejects "every form of proselytism."
The 2024 *Bishop of Rome* document recommends "re-reception" of Vatican I in light of Vatican II and explicitly frames *Pastor Aeternus*'s dogmatic definitions as a "significant obstacle" to ecumenism.

**12.
ARGUMENT — The structural inversion.** The SSPX's planned July 1, 2026 consecrations:
- explicitly do *not* claim to give the four new bishops any jurisdiction (per the perennial order/jurisdiction distinction — §7);
- are framed by Fr. Pagliarani (February 2026) as auxiliaries who will "lay down their episcopate before the pope" once the crisis ends (already in §10 from ep04 and ep06);
- thus structurally claim *less* than what Rome already ratifies in the CPA case (jurisdiction conferred without papal mandate and then accepted) or treats as already-realized communion in the Anglican / Orthodox cases.

The hypocrisy charge: Rome cannot coherently treat the SSPX consecrations as schismatic while treating each of the three other categories as ecumenical partners — unless schism no longer has the pre-1988 doctrinal content the charge presupposes.

This is the substantive answer to Cardinal Fernández's May 13, 2026 DDF statement (already in §10 from ep06) characterizing the planned consecrations as "a schismatic act."
